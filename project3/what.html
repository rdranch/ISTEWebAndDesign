In 2019, this 315 billion-tonne iceberg broke off of Antartica.

Normally, this wouldn't be possible unless an unusual event happened. In this case, global warming.

Similar events have happened before this time and since that time. These event will continue to happen and will not stop.

Global warming, as denoted by Google, is "a gradual increase in the overall temperature of the earth's atmosphere generally attributed to the greenhouse effect caused by increased levels of carbon dioxide, chlorofluorocarbons, and other pollutants."

The effect of global warming is not just limited to these glaciers. Ice on rivers and lakes is breaking up earlier, plant and animal ranges have shifted and trees are flowering sooner. There is an accelerated sea level rise and longer, more intense heat waves.
